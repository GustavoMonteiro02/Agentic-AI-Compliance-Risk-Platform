# GDPR Article-Level Extract

These source-linked records are designed for governance testing and citation-aware retrieval. They summarize selected official GDPR provisions and include locators for audit traceability. They are not a complete copy of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and are not legal advice.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_5 Principles relating to processing of personal data
Category: data protection principles
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 5
Tags: gdpr, personal data, lawfulness, fairness, transparency, minimisation, accuracy, retention, integrity, accountability
Personal data processing should follow core principles including lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, confidentiality, and accountability.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_6 Lawfulness of processing
Category: lawful basis
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 6
Tags: gdpr, lawful basis, consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interests
Processing personal data requires a valid lawful basis. Common governance checks include whether the processing is based on consent, contract necessity, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests where applicable.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_9 Processing of special categories of personal data
Category: special category data
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 9
Tags: gdpr, special category, health data, biometric data, sensitive data, safeguards
Special categories of personal data require heightened governance. Assessment should identify whether sensitive data is processed, whether an Article 9 condition applies, and what safeguards reduce risk to data subjects.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_22 Automated individual decision-making
Category: automated decision-making
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 22
Tags: gdpr, automated decision-making, profiling, human intervention, contestability, explanation
Automated individual decisions with legal or similarly significant effects require careful review. Governance evidence should cover human intervention, contestability, meaningful information about the logic involved, and safeguards for the rights and interests of individuals.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_30 Records of processing activities
Category: records of processing
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 30
Tags: gdpr, records, processing activities, accountability, audit
Controllers and processors should maintain records of processing activities where required. Relevant evidence includes purposes, categories of data subjects and data, recipients, retention periods, security measures, and responsibility ownership.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_32 Security of processing
Category: security
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 32
Tags: gdpr, security, confidentiality, integrity, availability, resilience, encryption, testing
Security measures should be appropriate to risk. Controls can include encryption, confidentiality, integrity, availability, resilience, restore capability, and regular testing or evaluation of technical and organisational measures.

### GDPR_ARTICLE_35 Data protection impact assessment
Category: data protection impact assessment
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj
Effective Date: 2018-05-25
Locator: Article 35
Tags: gdpr, dpia, high risk, automated processing, profiling, risk assessment
A data protection impact assessment should be considered where processing is likely to result in high risk to individuals. Governance evidence should include necessity and proportionality, risks to rights and freedoms, and measures to address those risks.
