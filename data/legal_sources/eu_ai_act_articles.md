# EU AI Act Article-Level Extract

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_9 Risk management system
Category: risk management
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 9
Tags: eu ai act, risk management, high-risk ai, controls, lifecycle
High-risk AI systems should be supported by a risk management system operated across the system lifecycle. The process should identify, estimate, evaluate, and mitigate reasonably foreseeable risks, including risks emerging from intended use and reasonably foreseeable misuse.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_10 Data and data governance
Category: data governance
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 10
Tags: eu ai act, data governance, training data, validation data, testing data, bias
High-risk AI systems that use data for training, validation, or testing should be supported by data governance and management practices. Relevant controls include data quality, relevance, representativeness, bias consideration, and documentation of assumptions and limitations.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_11 Technical documentation
Category: documentation
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 11
Tags: eu ai act, technical documentation, audit, conformity, system card
High-risk AI systems should have technical documentation prepared before market placement or service use. Documentation should be sufficient for assessment of conformity and should describe the system, intended purpose, design, development, validation, monitoring, and controls.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_12 Record keeping
Category: record keeping
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 12
Tags: eu ai act, logs, records, traceability, audit trail
High-risk AI systems should enable automatic logging where appropriate for their intended purpose. Logs should support traceability, monitoring, post-market oversight, incident investigation, and audit review.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_13 Transparency and instructions for use
Category: transparency
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 13
Tags: eu ai act, transparency, instructions, users, deployers, limitations
High-risk AI systems should be designed and accompanied by instructions that allow deployers to understand and use the system appropriately. Relevant information includes intended purpose, performance characteristics, limitations, human oversight measures, and reasonably foreseeable misuse.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_14 Human oversight
Category: human oversight
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 14
Tags: eu ai act, human oversight, high-risk ai, intervention, monitoring
High-risk AI systems should be designed and developed so that natural persons can effectively oversee their operation. Oversight measures should support understanding of system capabilities and limitations, monitoring, intervention, and safe stopping or override where appropriate.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_15 Accuracy robustness and cybersecurity
Category: security testing
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 15
Tags: eu ai act, accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity, resilience, testing
High-risk AI systems should achieve appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity. Technical and organizational measures should address errors, faults, inconsistencies, malicious attacks, and attempts to alter system behavior or performance.

### EU_AI_ACT_ARTICLE_16 Provider obligations
Category: provider obligations
Jurisdiction: EU
Document Type: regulation
Authority: European Union
Source URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
Effective Date: 2024-08-01
Locator: Article 16
Tags: eu ai act, provider, obligations, quality management, conformity, corrective action
Providers of high-risk AI systems should implement obligations that support conformity, documentation, quality management, corrective action, logging, transparency, and cooperation with competent authorities.
